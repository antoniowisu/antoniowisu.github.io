Bali

Bali is a province in Indonesia with Denpasar as its capital. Apart from consisting of the island of Bali, the province of Bali also consists of the surrounding small islands namely, Nusa Penida Island, Nusa Lembongan Island, Nusa Ceningan Island, Serangan Island and Menjangan Island. Bali is known as the Island of the Gods and the Island of a Thousand Temples. Bali is also famous for its beautiful tourist spots. Examples of tourist attractions in Bali are Kintamani, Ubud, Singaraja, Kuta and many other tourist attractions.

Bromo

Bromo is a mountain located in East Java, Indonesia. Mount Bromo has a height of 2,329 meters above sea level. Mount Bromo is famous as the most popular tourist object in East Java. For residents around Mount Bromo, the Tengger tribe believes that Mount Bromo is a holy mountain. Once a year the Tengger people hold a Yadnya Kasada or Kasodo ceremony. This ceremony takes place in a temple which is under the foot of Mount Bromo and continues to the top of Bromo.

Danau Toba

Lake Toba is a large natural lake in Indonesia which is in the caldera of Mount Supervolcano. The lake is 100 kilometers long, 30 kilometers wide and 508 meters deep. This lake is located in the center of the northern part of the island of Sumatra with a surface elevation of about 900 meters. Most of the people living around Lake Toba are Batak. Traditional Batak houses can be recognized by the shape of the roof (the edges curve up like a boat) and bright colors. Some residents living around Lake Toba believe that Lake Toba was created by a man who did not keep his promise to his wife. The whole village sank and became Lake Toba. The wife again transformed into a giant goldfish which is believed to exist until now.

Kelimutu

Kelimutu is a volcano located on Flores Island, NTT Province, Indonesia. This mountain has three lakes in the crater at its peak. This lake is known as the Three Colors Lake because it has three different colors, namely red, blue and white. Even so, these colors always change over time. Kelimutu is a combination of the words "keli" which means mountain and the word "quality" which means to boil. According to the beliefs of local residents, the colors on the Kelimutu lake have their own meaning and have very powerful natural forces. Residents around Lake Kelimutu believe that when the lake changes color, they must make offerings to the spirits of those who have died.

Jakarta

The Special Capital Region of Jakarta (DKI Jakarta) is the national capital and the largest city in Indonesia. According to the Indonesian administrative division system, Jakarta is a province with special regional status. Meanwhile, according to the general understanding, Jakarta is a metropolitan city. Jakarta is located on the coast of the northwestern part of Java Island. Previously known by several names including Sunda Kelapa, Jayakarta, and Batavia. As a center for business, politics and culture, Jakarta is home to the headquarters of state-owned enterprises, private companies and foreign companies. The city is also the seat of government agencies and the ASEAN secretariat office. Labuan Bajo

Labuan Bajo is a city in the Komodo sub-district, West Manggarai Regency, East Nusa Tenggara province, Indonesia. The people of West Manggarai district consist of various tribes, including the Majority of the Manggarai Tribe, and other tribes. Meanwhile in Labuan Bajo, the majority of the population is the West Manggarai tribe. The language used, apart from the official national language, Indonesian, is also used in everyday life by the West Manggarai dialect.

Maluku

Maluku is a province covering the southern part of the Maluku Islands, Indonesia. Before the colonial period, Maluku was the axis of the world's spice trade with cloves and nutmeg as the main merchandise. This made Maluku nicknamed the "Spice Islands" to this day. The Maluku people traded with traders from various regions in the archipelago and abroad such as Chinese, Arab and European traders. This wealth of spices also became an attraction for European nations who eventually controlled Maluku, starting with the Portuguese and finally the Dutch.

Raja Ampat

Raja Ampat Regency is one of the regencies in the province of Southwest Papua, Indonesia. The district capital is located in Waisai. This district has 610 islands, including the Raja Ampat archipelago. Four of them, namely Misool Island, Salawati, Batanta and Waigeo, are large islands. Of the entire island, only 35 islands are inhabited, while the other islands are uninhabited and most of them do not have names. Raja Ampat is also known as a paradise from the east because of its truly attractive natural beauty.
